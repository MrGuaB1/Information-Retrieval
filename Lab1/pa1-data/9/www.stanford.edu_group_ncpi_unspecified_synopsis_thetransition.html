synopsis the transition from initial education to working life in the united states of america this report part of a fourteen nation project sponsored by the organisation for economic co operation and development oecd describes the transition of us students from education to working life and the nature of the networks that support this process in addition to exploring the conditions under which students move from school to work and often back again the report examines this transition from the perspective of the employer asking to what degree employers participate in school to work partnerships and to what degree this participation benefits both the student and the employer the report also offers a summary of important federal and local school to work initiatives and provides a comprehensive demographic portrait of the student consumers of postsecondary education including breakdowns by gender race ethnicity school location socioeconomic class program of study and test performance

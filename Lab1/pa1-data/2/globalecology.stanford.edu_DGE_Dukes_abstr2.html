js dukes abstract 2 does global change increase the success of biological invaders dukes js mooney ha 1999 trends in ecology and evolution 14 4 135 139 biological invasions are gaining attention as a major threat to biodiversity and an important element of global change recent research indicates that other components of global change such as increases in nitrogen deposition and atmospheric co2 concentration favor groups of species that share certain physiological or life history traits new evidence suggests that many invasive species share traits that will allow them to capitalize on the various elements of global change increases in the prevalence of some of these biological invaders would alter basic ecosystem properties in ways that feed back to affect many components of global change

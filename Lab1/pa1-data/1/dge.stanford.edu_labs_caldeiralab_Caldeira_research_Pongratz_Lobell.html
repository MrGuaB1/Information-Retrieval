sunlight from the earth to reduce the rising global temperature have been met with concerns about the stability of food and water supplies worldwide in this modeling study we found that solar radiation geoengineering in a high co2 climate generally caused crop yields to increase however the most important step to protect global food security is to reduce greenhouse gas emissions pongratz j away from the earth could diminish the amount of climate change in a high co2 world however it has been suggested that this diminution would come at the cost of threatening the food and water supply for billions of people here we carry out high co2 geoengineering and control simulations using two climate models to predict the effects on global crop yields we find that in our models solar radiation geoengineering in a high co2 climate generally causes crop yields to increase largely because temperature stresses are diminished while the benefits of co2 fertilization are retained

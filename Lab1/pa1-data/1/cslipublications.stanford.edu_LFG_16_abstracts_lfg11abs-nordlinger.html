rachel nordlinger lfg11 abstract lfg and language documentation rachel nordlinger abstract proceedings of lfg11 csli publications on line the field of language documentation eg gippert himmelman and mosel 2006 has gathered momentum over the last 10 years with a focus on recording and preserving a comprehensive range of primary language data in the face of ever increasing global language endangerment evans 2009 in this paper i discuss recent developments in language documentation and consider the potential for interaction with lfg research drawing on my own fieldwork on murrinh patha and other language documentation projects while lfg has a long history of typologically informed theoretical work i argue for greater engagement with the field of language documentation for the mutual benefit of both research paradigms

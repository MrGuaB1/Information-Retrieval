initial labor market conditions and long term outcomes initial labor market conditions and long term outcomes for economists by paul oyer journal of economic perspectives 20 summer 2006 143 160 abstract each year graduate students entering the academic job market worry that they will suffer due to uncontrollable macroeconomic risk given the importance of general human capital and the relative ease of publicly observing productivity in academia one might expect unlucky graduating cohorts long term labor market outcomes to resemble those who graduate in favorable climates in this paper i analyze the relationship between macroeconomic conditions at graduation initial job placement and long term outcomes for phd economists from seven programs using macro conditions as an instrument for initial placement i show a causal effect of quality and type of initial job on long term job characteristics i also show that better initial placement increases research productivity which helps to limit the set of economic models that can explain the effect of initial placement on long term jobs back to paul oyer's page

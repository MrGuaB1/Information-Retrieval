newsletter alumni k 12 teachers creees international film series 2011 12 creees is pleased to announce that this year's international film series has been awarded a $15000 terra nova arts programming and events grant by the selection committee for the stanford institute for creativity & the arts sica the series was chosen from among a large number of proposals the award letter states that the project was selected because of its contribution to a crucial part of the mission of the arts initiative and sica encouraging innovative arts programming that will bring creativity and the arts more fully into the broader life of the campus this year's series will include four films each highlighting a different setting within our vast region 1930's serbia the fall of the ussr in moscow wartime afghanistan and divided germany each screening will be followed by a q&a session with the film's director see the printable flyer here all films shown in english or with english subtitles free and open to the public october 6 maratonci trce posacni krug the marathon family 1982 directed by slobodan sijan 92 minutes the topalovic family has been in the burial business for generations when the old 150 yrs old pantelija dies five generations of his heirs start to fight for the inheritance co sponsored by sica and the department of art & art history film studies november 30 my perestroika 2010 directed by robin hessman 88 minutes my perestroika tells the story of five people from the last generation of soviet children who were brought up behind the iron curtain just coming of age when the ussr collapsed they witnessed the world of their childhood crumble and change beyond recognition through the lives of these former schoolmates this intimate film reveals how they have adjusted to their post soviet reality in today's moscow co sponsored by sica and the hoover institution february 15 fixer the taking of ajmal naqshbandi 2009 directed by ian olds 84 minutes in 2007 the taliban kidnapped 24 year old ajmal naqshbandi and an italian journalist naqshbandi was one of afghanistan's best fixers someone hired by foreign journalists to facilitate translate and gain access for their stories co sponsored by sica and the ethics and war series april 10 krolilk po berlinsku rabbit a la berlin 2009 directed by bartek konopka 52 minutes filmmaker bartek konopka examines the history of the berlin wall as seen from the perspective of the rabbits who made their home in the no man's land between the two massive monoliths separating east and west germany when the walls come crumbling down the rabbits are uncertain how to handle their newfound freedom this film received an oscar nomination for best documentary short co sponsored by sica synopses courtesy of imdb com and fandango

lane library ways to give find a person research regenerative medicine regenerative medicine seeks to understand how and why stem cells differentiate into specialized tissues and to harness this potential for a wide variety of medical applications advances in regenerative medicine have the potential to prevent birth defects retard damage to diseased tissues repair injured tissues enhance the metabolic or biomechanical function of tissues and manipulate normal and abnormal tissue growth processes research in regenerative medicine occurs from the molecular level to clinical applications ongoing research at stanford includes genetic regulation of signaling systems involved in cell proliferation differentiation and patterning of tissues tissue engineering for the treatment of osteoarthritis understanding how mechanical loading affects the growth development regeneration and aging of skeletal tissues enhancing neurogenesis through electrical and chemical stimulation development of biologically based therapies to prevent or reduce pathological scar formation application of stem cell technologies to in vitro tissue production and the generation of specific cell types for therapeutic use bioengineering faculty members working in this area are jennifer cochran karl deisseroth christopher jacobs or see the jacobs lab page james swartz or see the swartz lab page

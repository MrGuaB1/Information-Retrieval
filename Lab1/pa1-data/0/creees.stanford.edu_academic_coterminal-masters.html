alumni k 12 teachers coterminal master's degree in russian east european & eurasian studies stanford undergraduates in the junior or senior year may apply for a coterminal bachelor's master's degrees this generally entails an additional year of study at stanford beyond the usual four years admission standards for coterms are the same as for external applicants to the reees ma program including the 3 year minimum of area language study completed prior to the coterm year coterms are also expected to have already completed significant coursework in the area and explain how continued study of reees at stanford will benefit them academically and or professionally coterminal candidates are required to take the gre and submit 3 letters of recommendation a statement of purpose transcripts and a writing sample for complete application requirements for the ma degree in reees click here coterm candidates who are us citizens are encouraged to apply for the academic year flas fellowship for advanced language study during the coterm year more information about the reees coterm degree may be found in the stanford bulletin coterminal degree candidates must submit the four page application to the registrar in lieu of the standard application for external candidates you will find the application posted on the registrar's office website other application materials including transcripts statement of purpose writing sample gre scores and letters of recommendation may be hand delivered or mailed to the center at the following address creees 417 galvez encina hall west 203 2nd floor

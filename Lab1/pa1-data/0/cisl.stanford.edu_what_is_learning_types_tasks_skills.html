activity it involves the very real hands on physical interaction of clinicians with patients much of patient care requires the skilled conduct of technical procedures whether as simple as performing an eye examination or drawing blood or as complex as performing laparoscopic surgery or placing a stent in clogged blood vessel a high skill level is important no one is born knowing how to do these tasks or the many other procedures needed in healthcare the skill for such tasks is only learned by practice simulation techniques can play an important role in providing this practice in a systematic and optimized way without any risk to real patients acquiring skill at procedural tasks is of great importance for early learners students or interns and residents but is also important for experienced personnel who need practice at new procedures as they are introduced or with existing tasks and procedures that are not performed frequently honing one's skills at tasks and procedures can be enhanced when the performance on them can be measured using various metrics such as time on task error rate or coordination such metrics may eventually allow the formative assessment of learners as they progress in skill and the establishment of benchmarks of achievement for both inexperienced and experienced personnel alike different sorts of tasks and skills can be simulated using different kinds of immersive and simulation modalities for example standardized patients are actors trained to present with the story and symptoms of specific diseases part task physical trainers reproduce the key portions of a procedure full simulators integrate physical procedures with decision making and teamwork the use of an appropriate mix of these devices can optimize learning and maintenance of skill for users at all levels of experience you are here stanford medicine school of medicine cisl what is isl types of learning a division of irt navigation for this section what is isl overview

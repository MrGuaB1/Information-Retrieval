when prompted have ideas they believe would make a positive difference in the world this year we challenged students to share such ideas in public and see if they can enroll others in their vision through an initiative called lowkeynotes the effort provides an opportunity for students to develop and practice a 15 minute keynote style presentation about an idea they think could change lives organizations or the world developed as a collaborative effort between the center for leadership and the school's mastery in communication initiative lowkeynotes provides a development opportunity for students as well as a platform to inspire members of the gsb community to tackle big problems and challenge the status quo students have the chance to grapple with some of the challenges that leaders face as communicators as well as what it takes to go from being just a good presenter to being an effective leader participating students will make presentations which touch a range of topics and are open to the public lowkeynotes encore event friday may 4 a smaller group of students selected by their peers and participating staff will be invited to present to a larger audience as part of alumni

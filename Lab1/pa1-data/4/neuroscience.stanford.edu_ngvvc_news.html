creation and support of certain specialized research capabilities would be a boon for neuroscientists accordingly the sintn strategic plan called for four service cores including three already in existence the advanced neuroscience imaging core the neuroscience behavior phenotyping and pharmacology core and the engineering shop core now the fourth leg of this technological platform has been established in the neuroscience gene vector and virus core mike lochrie phd viral vectors offer a way to deliver genes into cells that is more efficient and less toxic than a lot ofother methods says michael lochrie phd who has been hired to start up and run the core particularly in vivo there aren t a lot of other methods that are very good for instance he notes chemical or physical methods of moving genes into cells such as lipofection gene guns or using an electric field are difficult to use in a live animal and may damage cells in addition other methods most often result in short term gene expression measured in days whereas many viral methods can result in long term expression on the order of years lochrie says virus vectors can also be faster and cheaper constructing transgenic mice can produce animals with genes that are well integrated into the genome but can take six months or more and cost $5000 $10000 to develop with a viral vector you can introduce the gene into mice for less than $1000 in a few weeks lochrie says researchers could create and use viral vectors themselves lochrie says but it is not easy there is a big learning curve and some scientists don t have the expertise or even the facilities which is why i m here to help lochrie has long experience with viruses trained in molecular biology he did his post doc in harold varmus virology laboratory at ucsf and has also worked with viral vectors at avigen inc the virus core has been producing viruses since february 2010 and is constantly busy he attributes this in part to the convenience of being on campus the fast turnaround time he can offer and low costs i m finding that we are pretty competitive with other academic cores as well as companies around the country he says lochrie ultimately envisions a facility that is always evolving and developing like any other science there are always new capabilities and technologies that come along he says mike lochrie can be contacted by email

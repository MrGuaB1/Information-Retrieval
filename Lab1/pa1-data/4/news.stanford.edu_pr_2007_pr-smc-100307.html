september 28 2007 contact carol jump stanford management company 650 926 0258 stanford management company report issued the stanford merged pool mp generated a 23.0 percent investment return for the 12 months ended june 30 2007 according to the stanford management company smc over this one year period the us equity market as measured by the s&p 500 index was up 20.6 percent and the us bond market as measured by the lehman aggregate bond index was up 6.1 percent investment returns over the last year were particularly strong in international markets private equity and real estate investments over the past 10 years the mp has achieved an annualized return of 15.1 percent growing from $4.3 billion to $18.9 billion the mp is stanford's primary investment pool and includes the university's endowment and most of its expendable funds as well as capital reserves from stanford hospital and clinics and lucile packard children's hospital the mp is distinguished from the endowment in that it includes expendable funds and capital reserves stanford's endowment increased from $14.1 billion to about $17.2 billion for the fiscal year ending aug 31 2007 in june the university's board of trustees approved an increase in the target payout rate of the university's endowment from 5.0 percent to 5.5 percent to help fund important university capital improvement projects over the next several years this increase will take effect in the new fiscal year that began sept 1 our successive strong years of returns have direct bearing on stanford's ability to deliver on its far reaching education and research vision said john powers chief executive officer of the stanford management company we are pleased with our results for the june 30 2007 year the team at smc has really come together over that period of time and these results speak to the quality of their efforts powers said over the past five years ended june 2007 the stanford portfolio achieved an annualized return of 17.7 percent while over the same period the us stock market gained on average 10.7 percent per year this strong absolute and relative performance over a longer measurement horizon is a testament to

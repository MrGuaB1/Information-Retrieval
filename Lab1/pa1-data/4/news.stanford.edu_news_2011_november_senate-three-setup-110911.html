senate to meet thursday speakers include patricia gumport vice provost for graduate education and julie lythcott haims dean of freshmen and undergraduate advising at its third meeting of the academic year the faculty senate will hear a 30 minute presentation by patricia gumport vice provost for graduate education a 15 minute q&a will follow her presentation the office of the vice provost for graduate education works collaboratively with stanford's schools and departments to enhance the quality of graduate education at stanford university the senate also will hear a 20 minute presentation on undergraduate advising by julie lythcott haims dean of freshmen and undergraduate advising a 15 minute q&a will follow her presentation the office of undergraduate advising and research oversees pre major advising pre professional advising undergraduate research grants the transition of freshmen and transfer students and academic standing and petitions the office is dedicated to introducing freshmen to the full intellectual richness of undergraduate study at stanford supporting their academic and intellectual pursuits and instilling a deep sense of identity within and belonging to the university's community of scholars the process begins with the pairing of each freshman with two advisers a pre major adviser and an academic adviser the agenda for the nov 10 meeting including documents submitted by the speakers is available on the faculty senate website the meeting will begin at 3 15 pm in room 180 of the law school discussion is limited to members of the senate but members of the stanford community may request to attend the meeting by contacting the academic secretary's office at 723 4992 or trish del pozzo at delpozzo stanford edu share this story related to

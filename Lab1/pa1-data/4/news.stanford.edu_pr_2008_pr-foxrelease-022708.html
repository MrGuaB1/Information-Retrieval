news stanford edu news release february 27 2008 contact judith paulus associate director for communications fsi jpaulus stanford edu 650 723 8490 vicente fox former president of mexico to give public lecture march 5 vicente fox the former president of mexico who served from 2000 to 2006 will give a talk at stanford titled economic growth poverty and democracy in latin america a president's perspective from 4 30 to 6 pm wednesday march 5 in the stanford graduate school of business's bishop auditorium the address is free and open to the public and is the 2008 robert g wesson lecture in international relations theory and practice offered by stanford's freeman spogli institute for international studies fsi it is co sponsored this year by the graduate school of business fox ran for the presidency in 2000 as the candidate of the national action party pan on a platform focused on ending corruption and improving the economy and was the first to defeat the institutional revolutionary party pri which had governed mexico for more than 70 years a former rancher businessman and chief executive of coca cola in mexico fox devoted his efforts as president to expanding trade with the united states promoting economic growth and job creation and reducing corruption crime and drug trafficking since leaving office fox has been involved with a sweeping initiative to construct a social agenda for democracy in latin america for the next 20 years launched by alejandro toledo former president of peru from 2001 to 2006 toledo is a payne distinguished visiting lecturer at stanford this year it is a pleasure to welcome my friend former president vicente fox to stanford the freeman spogli institute and the center on democracy development and the rule of law where serious scholars and practitioners are committed to develop democracy that delivers concrete results for the poor and fosters social inclusion said toledo the work here at fsi accords with a bold new initiative which 12 former presidents of latin america have so generously supported to map out a 20 year agenda to stimulate economic growth and reduce the poverty inequality and social exclusion holding back human development in the region we hope many will come to hear president fox's address fsi director coit d blacker and toledo will give opening remarks toledo will join fox for the question and answer session at the conclusion of the lecture 30 editor note media are invited to attend and pose questions to fox following the formal lecture and q&a session please register with judith paulus fsi

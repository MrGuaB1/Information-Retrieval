about widescope what is widescope building consensus for budgets widescope is a stanford research project to help societies explore hard economic trade offs through data driven social networks our 1st experiment is this website help you explore federal & state budgets and search study budgets proposed by politicians and regular citizens propose design your own budgets and state your reasons influence comment vote develop consensus our philosophy a widescope will help users and societies see the bigger picture zoom beyond individual variables and observe overall objectives and feasibility our goals include using social networks to engage users about important data driven decisions with common data and shared discussion we believe it is easier to make progress on hard societal problems also we are building general purpose theoretical constructs and systems to apply our widescope philosophy in broader problems our system for budgets we have a database of budgets for federal 2011 for many states we have budgets for 2011 the rest are from 2009 or 2010 each budget has a set of categories for simplicity we chose easy to understand category names eg education defense corporation income taxes each category has a set of proposals changes for voters to support or modify an example for category higher education a simple proposal could be increase student scholarships including pell grants to reach another 50k students cost $100 m in our system anyone can make proposals funding we gratefully acknowledge funding from the national science foundation and support from amazon

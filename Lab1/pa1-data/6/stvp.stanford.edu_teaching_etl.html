entrepreneurial thought leader lectures the dfj entrepreneurial thought leaders program is a weekly lecture series where entrepreneurial leaders share the lessons of experience with the stanford community and anyone interested in entrepreneurship anywhere in the world examples of past speakers include founders of google jet blue surromed and yahoo venture capitalists john doerr steve jurvetson and vinod khosla executives from market leaders ciso electronic arts hp and nvidia social entrepreneurs the global fund for women and craigslist leading authors on high tech entrepreneurship geoff moore and guy kawasaki during the academic school year the course meets every wednesday from 4 30 5 30 pm in nvidia auditorium huang engineering center to view this quarter's lineup of speakers visit the dfj etl page on the stvp blog or download this quarter's etl flyer pdf 1.6 mb for additional course information please visit the etl website the entrepreneurial thought leaders program is brought to you by bases and the stanford technology ventures program the program is sponsored by draper fisher jurvetson stanford center for professional development each lecture is broadcast live on sitn channel e2 and via stanford online in addition all presentations during the quarter are archived and made available through stanford online itunes and stvp's entrepreneurship corner 2012 stanford

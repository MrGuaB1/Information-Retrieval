combination of ideas made an elegant mathematical system as well as a practical programming language then mathematical neatness became a goal and led to pruning some features from the core of the language this was partly motivated by esthetic reasons and partly by the belief that it would be easier to devise techniques for proving programs correct if the semantics were compact and without exceptions the results of cartwright 1976 and cartwright and mccarthy 1978 which show that lisp programs can be interpreted as sentences and schemata of first order logic provide new confirmation of

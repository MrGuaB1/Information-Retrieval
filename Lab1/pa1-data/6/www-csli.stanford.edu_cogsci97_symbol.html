the labyrinth of thought the labyrinth of thought the unofficial logo for the nineteenth annual conference of the cognitive science society is the labyrinth which in some religions symbolizes passage through life and which can also serve to represent the sequential aspects of human cognition the design for this particular labyrinth comes from the floor of chartes cathedral but the pattern also occurs elsewhere including grace church in san francisco note that unlike the mythical labyrinth of crete this design has no branch points making it a poor analog for the problem space hypothesis on the other hand it does look something like a brain at least in its convolutions and bilateral symmetry return to 1997 cognitive science conference page

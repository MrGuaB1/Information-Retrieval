report number cs tr 88 1195 institution stanford university department of computer science title a lower bound for radio broadcast author bar noy a author linial n author peleg d date february 1988 abstract a radio network is a synchronous network of processors that communicate by transmitting messages to their neighbors where a processor receives a message in a given step if and only if it is silent in this step and precisely one of its neighbors transmits in this paper we prove the existence of a family of radius 2 networks on n vertices for which any broadcast schedule requires at least omega log n log log n 2 rounds of transmissions this almost matches an upper bound of o log2 n rounds for networks of radius 2 proved earlier by bar yehuda goldreich and itai ftp reports stanford edu pub cstr reports cs tr 88 1195 cs tr 88 1195 pdf

a computationally discovered simplification of the ontological argument a computationally discovered simplification of the ontological argument authors paul e oppenheimer and edward n zalta reference australasian journal of philosophy 89 2 june 2011 333 349 abstract the authors investigate the ontological argument computationally the premises and conclusion of the argument are represented in the syntax understood by the automated reasoning engine prover9 using the logic of definite descriptions the authors developed a valid representation of the argument that required three non logical premises prover9 however discovered a simpler valid argument for god's existence from a single non logical premise reducing the argument to one non logical premise brings the investigation of the soundness of the argument into better focus also the simpler representation of the argument brings out clearly how the ontological argument constitutes an early example of a diagonal argument and moreover one used to establish a positive conclusion rather than a paradox preprint available online in pdf

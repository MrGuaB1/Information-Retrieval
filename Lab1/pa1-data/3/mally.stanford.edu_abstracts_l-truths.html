logical and analytic truths that are not necessary logical and analytic truths that are not necessary author edward n zalta reference the journal of philosophy 85 2 february 1988 57 74 abstract the author describes an interpreted modal language and produces some clear examples of logical and analytic truths that are not necessary these examples a are far simpler than the ones cited in the literature b show that a popular conception of logical truth in modal languages is incorrect and c show that there are contingent truths knowable a priori that do not depend on fixing the reference of a term preprint available online in pdf

report number csl tr 95 684 institution stanford university computer systems laboratory title performance area tradeoffs in booth multipliers author al twaijry hesham author flynn michael j date november 1995 abstract booth encoding is a method of reducing the number of summands required to produce the multiplication result this paper compares the performance area tradeoffs for the different booth algorithms when trees are used as the summation network this paper shows that the simple non booth algorithm is not a viable design and that currently booth 2 is the best design it also points out that in the future booth 3 may offer the best performance area ratio ftp reports stanford edu pub cstr reports csl tr 95 684 csl tr 95 684 pdf

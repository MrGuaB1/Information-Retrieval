saf 2012 brics in africa historically foreign trade and investment in africa have been dominated by european nations from pre colonial trading outposts to the colonial era and post colonial economic and trade linkages investment decisions regarding africa have hitherto been made in london paris lisbon and other european capitals however this trend has been on the decline with the increasing involvement of china and other bric nations on the continent the emergence of chinese investments and aid in africa has generated a lot of debate with opponents and advocates on both sides of the issue we intend to use this panel to examine these trends discuss their impacts on local economies and highlight the challenges for the united states and other western nations dates and times sat feb 25 2012

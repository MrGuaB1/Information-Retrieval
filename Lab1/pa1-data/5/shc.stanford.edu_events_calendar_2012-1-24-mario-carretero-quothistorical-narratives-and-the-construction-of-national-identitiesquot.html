center events mario carretero historical narratives and the construction of national identities tuesday january 24 2012 05 00 06 30 pm stanford humanities center levinthal hall bliss carnochan visitor memory construction and national identity are key issues for societies how can we believe and give sense to traditional narratives that explain the origins of nations and communities history taught at school plays a fundamental role in the construction of collective memory as childhood and adolescence are periods in life in which identity starts to form this talk will present the ways in which historical knowledge is understood by students from 12 to 18 years of age and by adults carretero will draw on research from his recent book constructing patriotism teaching history and memories in global worlds 2011 mario carretero is in residence at the center as the bliss carnochan distinguished visitor and a fsi humanities center international visitor carretero is professor of psychology at
